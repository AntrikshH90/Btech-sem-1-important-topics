### Unit 1: Environment & Ecosystem
**Q1. Define Ecosystem. Explain the structure and function of an ecosystem. (10 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Ecosystem:** A self-sustaining structural and functional unit of the biosphere consisting of living (biotic) and non-living (abiotic) components interacting with each other.

**Structure:**
1.  **Abiotic Components:**
    *   Physical: Temperature, Light, Soil.
    *   Chemical: Water, Oxygen, Minerals.
2.  **Biotic Components:**
    *   **Producers (Autotrophs):** Green plants (Photosynthesis).
    *   **Consumers (Heterotrophs):**
        *   Primary (Herbivores)
        *   Secondary (Carnivores)
        *   Tertiary (Top Carnivores)
    *   **Decomposers (Saprotrophs):** Bacteria, Fungi (Recycle nutrients).

**Functions:**
1.  **Energy Flow:** Unidirectional flow from Sun -> Producers -> Consumers.
2.  **Nutrient Cycling:** Biogeochemical cycles (Carbon, Nitrogen).
3.  **Food Chains/Webs:** Transfer of food energy.
4.  **Ecological Succession:** Gradual change in species composition.

**Q2. Differentiate between Food Chain and Food Web. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
| Feature | Food Chain | Food Web |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | Linear sequence of organisms where nutrients and energy are transferred. | Network of interconnected food chains. |
| **Pathways** | Single path. | Multiple paths. |
| **Stability** | Less stable (if one member is removed, chain breaks). | More stable (alternative food sources available). |
| **Example** | Grass -> Deer -> Lion | Grass -> Grasshopper -> Frog -> Snake -> Eagle. |

### Unit 2: Natural Resources
**Q1. What are Renewable and Non-renewable resources? Give examples. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
*   **Renewable Resources:** Resources that can be replenished naturally over a short period.
    *   *Examples:* Solar energy, Wind energy, Water, Forests (if managed sustainably).
*   **Non-renewable Resources:** Resources that exist in fixed amounts and cannot be replenished on a human time scale.
    *   *Examples:* Coal, Petroleum, Natural Gas, Minerals.

**Q2. Discuss the impact of over-exploitation of water resources. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
1.  **Lowering of Water Table:** Excessive pumping of groundwater.
2.  **Salinity:** Intrusion of saltwater in coastal areas.
3.  **Droughts:** Scarcity of water for agriculture and drinking.
4.  **Land Subsidence:** Sinking of land due to groundwater extraction.
5.  **Conflicts:** Water wars between states/countries (e.g., Cauvery dispute).

### Unit 3: Environmental Pollution
**Q1. Define Air Pollution. Discuss its causes, effects, and control measures. (10 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Air Pollution:** Presence of substances in the atmosphere that are harmful to the health of humans and other living beings, or cause damage to the climate or materials.

**Causes:**
1.  **Primary Pollutants:** Emitted directly (CO, SO2, NOx, Particulate Matter).
2.  **Secondary Pollutants:** Formed by reactions (Ozone, Smog, Acid Rain).

**Effects:**
1.  **Health:** Respiratory diseases (Asthma, Bronchitis), Cancer.
2.  **Environment:** Acid Rain, Global Warming, Ozone Depletion.
3.  **Materials:** Corrosion of buildings (Taj Mahal yellowing).

**Control Measures:**
1.  Use of electrostatic precipitators in industries.
2.  Switching to cleaner fuels (CNG, Solar, Wind).
3.  Planting trees (Green Belt).
4.  Strict emission norms (Bharat Stage VI).

### Unit 4: Current Environmental Issues
**Q1. Explain the phenomenon of Global Warming and its consequences. (10 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Global Warming:** The gradual increase in the average temperature of the Earth's atmosphere and oceans due to the Greenhouse Effect.

**Causes (Greenhouse Gases):**
*   Carbon Dioxide (CO2) - Burning fossil fuels.
*   Methane (CH4) - Agriculture, landfills.
*   Nitrous Oxide (N2O) - Fertilizers.
*   CFCs - Refrigerants.

**Consequences:**
1.  **Melting Ice Caps:** Rise in sea levels, coastal flooding.
2.  **Climate Change:** Extreme weather events (hurricanes, droughts).
3.  **Loss of Biodiversity:** Species extinction.
4.  **Agriculture:** Changing crop patterns, reduced yields.
5.  **Health:** Spread of tropical diseases (Malaria, Dengue).

### Unit 5: Environmental Protection & Social Issues
**Q1. Discuss the salient features of the Environment Protection Act, 1986. (10 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Environment Protection Act (1986):** Enacted after the Bhopal Gas Tragedy. It is an "Umbrella Legislation".

**Salient Features:**
1.  **Central Govt Powers:** Empowers Central Govt to take all measures to protect and improve the environment.
2.  **Standards:** Lay down standards for emission/discharge of pollutants.
3.  **Prohibitions:** Restrict industrial operations in sensitive areas.
4.  **Penalties:** Fine up to â‚¹1 Lakh or imprisonment up to 5 years, or both.
5.  **Entry & Inspection:** Power to enter and inspect any industrial unit.
6.  **Public Liability:** Handling of hazardous substances.

### Unit 1: Environment & Ecosystem
**Q1. Explain the concept of Ecological Pyramids. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Ecological Pyramid:** Graphical representation of the trophic structure and function of an ecosystem.

**Types:**
1.  **Pyramid of Number:** Represents the number of individuals at each trophic level. (Upright or Inverted).
2.  **Pyramid of Biomass:** Represents the total dry weight of living matter. (Upright or Inverted).
3.  **Pyramid of Energy:** Represents the amount of energy at each level. **ALWAYS Upright** (10% Law).

**Q2. What is the Multidisciplinary nature of Environmental Studies? (2 Marks)**
**Answer:**
Environmental studies deal with every issue that affects an organism. It is multidisciplinary because it includes:
*   **Life Sciences:** Biology, Botany.
*   **Physical Sciences:** Chemistry, Physics.
*   **Mathematics/Statistics:** Data analysis.
*   **Social Sciences:** Sociology, Economics.
*   **Law:** Environmental protection acts.

### Unit 2: Natural Resources
**Q1. What is Deforestation? What are its causes and effects? (10 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Deforestation:** Permanent removal of trees.

**Causes:**
1.  **Agriculture Expansion:** Slash and burn.
2.  **Urbanization:** Infrastructure development.
3.  **Logging:** Timber demand.
4.  **Mining:** Destruction of forest cover.

**Effects:**
1.  **Climate Change:** Increased CO2.
2.  **Soil Erosion:** Loss of topsoil.
3.  **Loss of Biodiversity:** Habitat destruction.
4.  **Floods:** Reduced water absorption.

**Q2. Explain the concept of Sustainable Development. (2 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Sustainable Development:** "Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."
*   **3 Pillars:** Economic, Social, Environmental.

### Unit 3: Environmental Pollution
**Q1. What is Solid Waste Management? Explain the 3R principle. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Solid Waste Management:** Collection, treatment, and disposal of solid waste.

**3R Principle:**
1.  **Reduce:** Minimize waste generation.
2.  **Reuse:** Use items again.
3.  **Recycle:** Process waste to make new products.

**Q2. Discuss the effects of Noise Pollution. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
1.  **Auditory Effects:** Temporary/Permanent hearing loss.
2.  **Non-Auditory Effects:** Hypertension, Sleep disturbance, Stress, Interference with communication.

### Unit 4: Current Environmental Issues
**Q1. What is Acid Rain? How is it formed? (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Acid Rain:** Rainfall with pH < 5.6.
**Formation:** SO2 and NOx react with water vapor to form Sulfuric and Nitric acid.
**Effects:** Damages buildings (Stone Leprosy), acidifies water bodies, damages forests.

**Q2. Discuss Ozone Layer Depletion. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**Cause:** CFCs release Chlorine which destroys Ozone (O3).
**Hole:** Prominent over Antarctica.
**Effects:** Skin Cancer, Cataracts, damage to marine life.
**Protocol:** Montreal Protocol.

### Unit 5: Environmental Protection & Social Issues
**Q1. Explain the role of NGOs in Environmental Protection. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
**NGOs:**
1.  **Awareness:** Public campaigns.
2.  **Watchdogs:** Monitoring policies.
3.  **Litigation:** Filing PILs.
4.  **Action:** Tree plantation, cleaning drives.
5.  *Examples:* WWF, Greenpeace.

**Q2. Discuss the impact of Population Growth on the Environment. (5 Marks)**
**Answer:**
1.  **Resource Depletion:** High demand for food/water.
2.  **Pollution:** Increased waste/vehicles.
3.  **Deforestation:** Land clearing for housing.
4.  **Urbanization:** Slum growth.
