# AKTU B.Tech 1st Semester Important Questions (2025 Exam)

**Target Audience:** B.Tech 1st Year Students (AKTU)
**Subjects Covered:** Mathematics-I, Physics, Basic Electrical, PPS, EVS

---

## 1. Engineering Mathematics-I (BAS-103)

### Unit 1: Matrices
1.  **Cayley-Hamilton Theorem:** State and prove Cayley-Hamilton theorem. Find the inverse of a matrix using this theorem.
2.  **Eigen Values & Eigen Vectors:** Find the eigen values and corresponding eigen vectors of a given 3x3 matrix.
3.  **Diagonalization:** Discuss the diagonalization of a square matrix.
4.  **Rank of Matrix:** Find the rank of a matrix using Echelon form or Normal form.
5.  **System of Linear Equations:** Test for consistency and solve the system of linear equations (Non-homogeneous).

### Unit 2: Differential Calculus-I
1.  **Leibnitz Theorem:** State Leibnitz theorem and find the nth derivative of functions like $x^2 e^{ax}$, $e^{a \sin^{-1}x}$, etc.
2.  **Euler's Theorem:** State Euler's theorem on homogeneous functions. Verify it for given functions.
3.  **Partial Differentiation:** If $u = \tan^{-1}(\frac{x^3+y^3}{x-y})$, prove specific relations using partial derivatives.
4.  **Curve Tracing:** Trace curves like $y^2(a-x) = x^3$ (Cissoid) or $r = a(1+\cos\theta)$ (Cardioid).

### Unit 3: Differential Calculus-II
1.  **Taylor’s & Maclaurin’s Series:** Expand functions like $\log(1+x)$, $\sin x$, or $e^x$ using Maclaurin's series. Expand $f(x,y)$ using Taylor's series.
2.  **Jacobians:** Define Jacobian. If $x = r\cos\theta, y = r\sin\theta$, find $\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(r,\theta)}$.
3.  **Maxima and Minima:** Find the extreme values of functions of two variables (e.g., $x^3 + y^3 - 3axy$).
4.  **Lagrange’s Method of Multipliers:** Discuss Lagrange’s method for finding maxima/minima with constraints.

### Unit 4: Multiple Integration
1.  **Double Integrals:** Evaluate double integrals over a given region.
2.  **Change of Order of Integration:** Change the order of integration in $\int_0^a \int_{x^2/a}^{2a-x} xy \, dy \, dx$ and evaluate.
3.  **Beta and Gamma Functions:** Define Beta and Gamma functions and the relation between them. Prove $\Gamma(1/2) = \sqrt{\pi}$.
4.  **Volume and Surface Area:** Find the volume bounded by coordinate planes and a plane/surface.

### Unit 5: Vector Calculus
1.  **Gradient, Divergence, Curl:** Find the directional derivative, unit normal vector, and angle between surfaces.
2.  **Gauss Divergence Theorem:** State and verify Gauss Divergence theorem for a given vector field.
3.  **Stokes’ Theorem:** State and verify Stokes' theorem.
4.  **Green’s Theorem:** Evaluate line integrals using Green's theorem.

## 2. Engineering Physics (BAS-101)

### Unit 1: Relativistic Mechanics
1.  **Lorentz Transformation:** Derive Lorentz transformation equations. Show that at low velocities, they reduce to Galilean transformations.
2.  **Time Dilation & Length Contraction:** Derive expressions for time dilation and length contraction.
3.  **Mass-Energy Equivalence:** Derive Einstein’s mass-energy relation $E = mc^2$.
4.  **Michelson-Morley Experiment:** Explain the experiment, its negative result, and conclusions.

### Unit 2: Electromagnetic Field Theory
1.  **Maxwell’s Equations:** Write Maxwell’s equations in integral and differential forms and explain their physical significance.
2.  **Poynting Vector:** State and prove Poynting theorem. Explain the physical significance of the Poynting vector.
3.  **EM Waves:** Derive the wave equation for electromagnetic waves in free space.

### Unit 3: Quantum Mechanics
1.  **Schrodinger Wave Equation:** Derive Time-Dependent and Time-Independent Schrodinger wave equations.
2.  **Particle in a 1D Box:** Solve the Schrodinger equation for a particle in a one-dimensional infinite potential box and find energy eigen values.
3.  **Compton Effect:** Explain Compton effect and derive the expression for Compton shift.
4.  **De-Broglie Hypothesis:** Discuss matter waves and Davisson-Germer experiment.

### Unit 4: Wave Optics
1.  **Newton’s Rings:** Explain the formation of Newton’s rings. Derive the expression for the diameter of bright and dark rings.
2.  **Diffraction Grating:** Explain the construction and theory of a plane transmission grating. Derive the condition for principal maxima.
3.  **Rayleigh’s Criterion:** State Rayleigh’s criterion for resolution. Find the resolving power of a grating.

### Unit 5: Fiber Optics & Laser
1.  **Optical Fibers:** Explain the principle of optical fiber (Total Internal Reflection). Derive expressions for Numerical Aperture and Acceptance Angle.
2.  **Types of Fibers:** Differentiate between Step-index and Graded-index fibers, Single-mode and Multi-mode fibers.
3.  **Laser Action:** Explain Spontaneous and Stimulated emission, Population Inversion, and Pumping.
4.  **He-Ne / Ruby Laser:** Describe the construction and working of He-Ne laser or Ruby laser with energy level diagrams.

## 3. Basic Electrical Engineering (BEE-101)

### Unit 1: DC Circuits
1.  **Circuit Theorems:** State and prove Thevenin’s Theorem, Norton’s Theorem, and Superposition Theorem with examples.
2.  **KCL & KVL:** Solve complex circuits using Mesh and Nodal analysis.
3.  **Star-Delta Transformation:** Derive the relationship for Star to Delta and Delta to Star transformation.

### Unit 2: AC Circuits
1.  **RLC Series Circuit:** Derive the expression for impedance, current, and power factor in a series R-L-C circuit. Discuss Resonance conditions.
2.  **Parallel Resonance:** Explain parallel resonance and derive the expression for resonant frequency.
3.  **3-Phase Systems:** Derive the relationship between Line voltage/current and Phase voltage/current in Star and Delta connections.

### Unit 3: Transformers
1.  **EMF Equation:** Derive the EMF equation of a single-phase transformer.
2.  **Equivalent Circuit:** Draw and explain the equivalent circuit of a transformer.
3.  **Efficiency & Regulation:** Explain efficiency and voltage regulation. Derivation for condition of maximum efficiency.
4.  **Losses:** Discuss Core and Copper losses.

### Unit 4: Electrical Machines
1.  **DC Machines:** Explain the construction and working principle of a DC Motor/Generator. Derive the EMF equation.
2.  **3-Phase Induction Motor:** Explain the construction (Squirrel cage vs Slip ring) and working principle (Rotating Magnetic Field).
3.  **Torque-Slip Characteristics:** Draw and explain the Torque-Slip characteristics of an Induction Motor.

### Unit 5: Electrical Installations
1.  **Protection Devices:** Explain the working of MCB, MCCB, ELCB, and Fuse.
2.  **Earthing:** What is earthing? Explain Plate and Pipe earthing methods.
3.  **Battery:** Types of batteries and their characteristics (Lead-acid, Li-ion).

## 4. Programming for Problem Solving (PPS - BCS-101)

### Unit 1: Basics & Control Flow
1.  **Block Diagram of Computer:** Draw and explain the block diagram of a digital computer.
2.  **Operators:** Explain various types of operators in C (Arithmetic, Relational, Logical, Bitwise, Ternary).
3.  **Type Conversion:** Differentiate between Implicit and Explicit type conversion.

### Unit 2: Control Statements
1.  **Loops:** Difference between `while`, `do-while`, and `for` loops with examples.
2.  **Switch Case:** Write a program to implement a simple calculator using switch-case.
3.  **Pattern Printing:** Programs to print pyramids, diamonds, Pascal's triangle.

### Unit 3: Arrays & Strings
1.  **Matrix Operations:** Write a program for Matrix Multiplication (very important).
2.  **Sorting & Searching:** Explain Bubble Sort and Linear/Binary Search with programs.
3.  **String Handling:** Explain string library functions (`strlen`, `strcpy`, `strcat`, `strcmp`) and write programs without using them.

### Unit 4: Functions & Pointers
1.  **Recursion:** Write a program to find Factorial or Fibonacci series using recursion.
2.  **Call by Value vs Reference:** Differentiate with examples (Swapping two numbers).
3.  **Pointers:** Explain pointer arithmetic. Write a program to sum array elements using pointers.

### Unit 5: Structures & File Handling
1.  **Structure vs Union:** Differentiate between Structure and Union with memory layout examples.
2.  **Linked List:** Define Linked List. Write a C program to insert and delete a node.
3.  **File Handling:** Write a program to copy the contents of one file to another. Explain file opening modes.
4.  **Macros:** What are macros? Explain types of macros.

## 5. Environment & Ecology (BAS-104)

### Unit 1: Environment & Ecosystem
1.  **Ecosystem:** Define Ecosystem. Explain the structure and function of an ecosystem (Producers, Consumers, Decomposers).
2.  **Food Chain & Web:** Differentiate between Food Chain and Food Web. Explain Ecological Pyramids.
3.  **Cycles:** Explain Nitrogen Cycle or Carbon Cycle with a diagram.

### Unit 2: Natural Resources
1.  **Water Resources:** Discuss the problems of Over-utilization of surface and ground water (Floods, Droughts).
2.  **Energy Resources:** Differentiate between Renewable and Non-renewable energy sources (Solar, Wind vs Coal, Petroleum).
3.  **Deforestation:** Causes and effects of deforestation.

### Unit 3: Biodiversity
1.  **Biodiversity:** Define Biodiversity. Explain Genetic, Species, and Ecosystem diversity.
2.  **Threats & Conservation:** What are the threats to biodiversity? Discuss In-situ and Ex-situ conservation methods.
3.  **Hotspots:** What are biodiversity hotspots? Name hotspots in India.

### Unit 4: Environmental Pollution
1.  **Air Pollution:** Causes, effects, and control measures of Air Pollution.
2.  **Water Pollution:** Sources and effects of Water Pollution. Explain BOD and COD.
3.  **Solid Waste Management:** Discuss the causes, effects, and control measures of urban and industrial solid waste.

### Unit 5: Social Issues & Environment
1.  **Global Warming & Climate Change:** Causes and effects of Greenhouse effect and Global warming.
2.  **Ozone Depletion:** Mechanism of Ozone layer depletion and its effects.
3.  **Acid Rain:** Causes and effects of Acid Rain.
4.  **Sustainable Development:** Define Sustainable Development and its goals.
5.  **Acts:** Brief note on Environment Protection Act, Air Act, or Water Act.
