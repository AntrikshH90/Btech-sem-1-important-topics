## üßÆ Mathematics-I (BAS-103)

### Unit 1: Matrices (‚úÖ You are strong here! - 2 Hr Limit)
- [ ] **Review:** Rapidly write formulas for Cayley-Hamilton & Eigen properties. (30 mins)
- [ ] **Complex Matrices:** Just verify definitions of Hermitian ($A^\theta = A$) & Unitary ($A^\theta A = I$). (30 mins)
- [ ] **Practice:** Solve 1 tricky "System of Equation" question for consistency. (1 hr)

### Unit 2: Differential Calculus-I (The "Pass or Fail" Topics)
*Strategy: Euler & Leibnitz are 70% of the unit. Mastering them allows you to skip Curve Tracing.*
- [ ] **Leibnitz Theorem:** **(MUST DO)** Practice $n^{th}$ derivative of $x^2 \cos x$ type problems.
- [ ] **Euler‚Äôs Theorem:** **(MUST DO)** Essential. Practice for $u = \sin^{-1}(\frac{x+y}{\sqrt{x}+\sqrt{y}})$.
- [ ] **Safety Net:** **Partial Derivatives:** Learn "Total Derivative" formula ($du = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}dx + \frac{\partial u}{\partial y}dy$). *Risk Reducer.*

### Unit 3: Differential Calculus-II (‚úÖ You are strong here!)
*Strategy: You already know the hard parts (Jacobian/Lagrange). Just add Series expansion to be 100% safe.*
- [ ] **Review:** Solve 1 Jacobian problem $J(u,v) = \frac{\partial(u,v)}{\partial(x,y)}$. (20 mins)
- [ ] **Review:** Check Lagrange steps for "Stationary Points". (15 mins)
- [ ] **The Gap:** **Maclaurin‚Äôs Series:** Memorize expansion for $\log(1+x)$ or $e^x \sin x$. (Important for short answers).

### Unit 4: Multiple Integration (The "Change of Order" Game)
- [ ] **Change of Order:** **(GUARANTEED)** Master the strip sliding method (Horizontal $\leftrightarrow$ Vertical). Practice 1 problem.
- [ ] **Beta-Gamma:** Prove $\Gamma(1/2) = \sqrt{\pi}$ and write the relation $\beta(m,n) = \frac{\Gamma m \Gamma n}{\Gamma (m+n)}$.
- [ ] **Dirichlet‚Äôs Integral:** Just memorize the formula for $\iiint V dx dy dz$ where $x+y+z \le 1$. (Quick 2 marks).

### Unit 5: Vector Calculus (Easy Formulas)
- [ ] **Divergence/Curl:** Find Grad ($\nabla \phi$), Div ($\nabla \cdot F$), and Curl ($\nabla \times F$).
- [ ] **Theorems:** Memorize **Gauss Divergence** statement ($\iint \to \iiint$) and **Stokes Theorem** statement ($\int \to \iint$).
