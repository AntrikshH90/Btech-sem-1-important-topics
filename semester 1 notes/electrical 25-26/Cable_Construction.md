## 2. Layers Explanation (C-I-M-B-A-S)

### 1. **C**ore (Conductor)
*   **Material:** Copper or Aluminum.
*   **Function:** Carrries the electric current.
*   **Type:** Can be single-core or multi-core (3, 3.5, or 4 cores). stranded to provide flexibility.

### 2. **I**nsulation
*   **Material:** Impregnated paper, Varnish Cambric, or Rubber/PVC.
*   **Thickness:** Depends on the **Voltage** level.
*   **Function:** Prevents leakage of current from conductor to earth or other phases.

### 3. **M**etallic Sheath
*   **Material:** Lead or Aluminum alloy.
*   **Function:** Protects the cable from **moisture** (hygroscopic protection), gases, and damaging liquids from the soil.
*   **Key:** Moisture is the biggest enemy of insulation!

### 4. **B**edding
*   **Material:** Fibrous material like Jute or Hessian tape.
*   **Function:** Acts as a cushion/buffer. Protects the **Metallic Sheath** from injury due to **Armouring**.

### 5. **A**rmouring
*   **Material:** Galvanized Steel Wire or Steel Tape.
*   **Function:** Provides **Mechanical Strength**. Protects cable from physical damage (like digging, rocks).
*   **Note:** Not required for lowvoltage cables but essential for high voltage/underground.

### 6. **S**erving
*   **Material:** Similar to bedding (Jute, PVC, or fibrous material).
*   **Function:** The outermost layer. Protects the **Armouring** from atmospheric conditions and corrosion.
