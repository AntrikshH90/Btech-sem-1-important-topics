**Q. Discuss the role and importance of Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) in environmental protection.**

### **Answer (Easy Version):**

**1. Definition:**
*   **NGO (Non-Governmental Organization):** Private, non-profit organizations that work for the welfare of society and the environment without government control.
*   They act as a **"Watchdog"** for the environment.

**2. Role & Functions of NGOs:**
> **Mnemonic:** **PLAR** (Like "Polar" Bear)
*   **P - Protection:** They physically protect forests and wildlife (e.g., stopping poaching).
*   **L - Legal Aid (PIL):** They file **Public Interest Litigations (PIL)** in courts against industries that pollute rivers or air.
*   **A - Awareness:** They educate people about pollution, recycling, and climate change through campaigns and slogans.
*   **R - Research:** They collect data on endangered species and pollution levels to help the government make better laws.

**3. Major Examples (Must write for marks):**
1.  **WWF (World Wide Fund for Nature):** works for wildlife conservation (Panda logo).
2.  **Greenpeace:** fights against nuclear testing and toxic waste.
3.  **BNHS (Bombay Natural History Society):** works for bird and wildlife safety in India.
4.  **Chipko Movement:** A people's movement (supported by NGOs) to save trees in Uttarakhand.

**4. Importance:**
*   Government cannot reach everywhere; NGOs fill the gap.
*   They organize **mass movements** (like "Save Narmada" or "Save Soil").

---
**Summary for Exam:**
*   **NGO** = Public "Watchdog".
*   **Functions** = **PLAR** (Protection, Legal, Awareness, Research).
*   **Examples** = WWF, Greenpeace.
